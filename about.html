How It Started

Three chapters. One ghee.

Some brands have a marketing story. We just have a real one — passed from a kitchen courtyard in 1962 to the jar that lands at your door.

Chapter 01 · 1962

It started with Aaji.

Our grandmother — we still call her Aaji — kept two native cows in the courtyard of our house in Solapur. Every winter, she made ghee the bilona way: cultured curd, hand-churned to butter, slow-simmered for two whole days on a low flame.

The smell of it filled three houses on our lane. Neighbours would walk over with empty steel boxes. She'd send them home full.

Chapter 02 · 2018

One day we realised it was disappearing.

Aaji's recipe was slipping out of family kitchens — replaced by industrial pouches with 12 ingredients we couldn't pronounce. Ghee with palm oil. Ghee with permitted colours. Ghee that didn't smell like ghee.

We knew what we had to do. Take her exact method. Build a small, clean, modern facility around it. Refuse to dilute. Refuse to shortcut. And share it with anyone who still cared about real ghee.

Chapter 03 · Today

2,400+ kitchens. Still made by hand.

Today, Godaivyam reaches 2,400+ families across India. Same recipe. Same 48-hour simmer. Same Solapur facility. Every jar that leaves our doors is one we'd serve our own kids.

We still test every single batch in independent FSSAI labs. We still source milk only from native-breed cows whose farmers we know by name. We still refuse to add anything that doesn't belong.

This is the promise we make on every jar — and the promise we'd love you to taste.
