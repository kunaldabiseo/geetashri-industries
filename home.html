Is Godaivyam real A2 cow ghee?

Yes — Godaivyam is made entirely from the milk of native-breed Indian cows, which produce A2 protein. We source only from small dairy partners in and around Solapur, Maharashtra — herds we've personally visited.

Our A2 status is verified by independent FSSAI labs for every production batch.

How is bilona ghee different from supermarket ghee?

Most mass-market ghee is made by directly heating cream and finished in hours. Godaivyam follows the traditional bilona method — cultured curd, hand-churned to butter, then slow-simmered for 48 hours.

The result is a richer aroma, deeper golden colour, and the classic granular texture you'll notice the first time you open the jar.

Can lactose-intolerant people eat Godaivyam ghee?

Most can. Our slow-simmering removes the milk solids — water, lactose and casein — leaving only pure butterfat. Many customers with dairy sensitivities use Godaivyam daily without any issue.

If you have a severe dairy allergy, please consult your doctor first.

How long does an opened jar last?

A sealed jar lasts 9 months from manufacture. Once opened, store in a cool dry place away from direct sunlight, always use a clean dry spoon, and it will stay fresh for 4–6 months easily.

Unlike most fats, ghee actually improves with age — older ghee is prized in Ayurveda.

What's the best way to use ghee in daily cooking?

Ghee has a high smoke point (~250°C), so it's brilliant for tadka, sautéing, deep-frying, baking and even drizzling over hot dal-chawal or roti. One teaspoon daily is the traditional Ayurvedic recommendation for adults.
